寂静的一秒間

1 Second of Silence

陈璐 | Chen Lu

The sense of "silence".

Scenery in vertical composition.

"Asaṃjñā" and "saṃjñā".

The Diamond Sutra mentioned about three ways categorizing "beings". One is to categorize them by the way they were born, such as egg-born, born from a womb, moisture-born, or miraculously born. Another one is categorizing "beings" by whether they have "forms". There is one more way to differentiate "beings" – whether they have "saṃjñā" (perception). "Beings" who live in and "rupa-loka" (the Fine-Material World) and "kama-loka" (the Sensuous World) are with "saṃjñā", while the rest have much milder perception, "asaṃjñā".

One after the other, "asaṃjñā" and "saṃjñā" appear, like breathing out, breathing in. The moment, in between of every breath, is the 1 second of silence.
